This section analyzes the difference in GDP per capita from 3 types of nations: 
- Western countries (more developed countries such as those in Western Europe, North America, etc.)
- Developing countries (countries in poorer regions (Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, etc.)
- Transitional countries, known as the BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa)

These will be compared via boxplots consisting of the country GDP per capita per group in 1960
and in 2010.
